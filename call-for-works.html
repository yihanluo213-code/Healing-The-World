Call for Artists & Students

Submit original artworks exploring the theme of Healing the World.

Submit Your Work

Theme

Healing the World - Art, Hope, and Shared Futures

This theme invites artists to reflect on how art can respond to the world we live in and the world we hope to create.

  • Personal experiences and emotional healing
  • Collective memory and cultural identity
  • The relationship between humans and nature
  • Conflict, migration, environment, and belonging
  • Visions of the future and expressions of hope

Artwork Requirements

  • JPG or PNG digital submission
  • Minimum resolution: 300 dpi
  • Original artworks only; no prints or reproductions
  • Painting, watercolor, oil, acrylic, illustration, or mixed media
  • Maximum size: 24 x 30 in
  • Each student or artist may submit 1-5 artworks

Submission & Selection Process

1

Digital Submission

Artists submit artwork images and details online for initial review.

2

Jury Review

Works are evaluated for relevance, creativity, originality, artistic quality, composition, and emotional impact.

3

Selection

Selected artworks will be invited to become part of the exhibition.

Jury Committee

Yan Baoyun

Artist

Yan Baoyun is a painter with over 20 years of artistic experience. He began his career in Beijing's Panjiayuan art district and later studied oil painting at Tianjin Academy of Fine Arts. Since moving to Canada in 2022, he has continued his artistic practice and teaching in White Rock.

Junyi Ji

Artist

Junyi Ji, also known as Alex Ji, holds a master's degree in oil painting from Tianjin Academy of Fine Arts and is a member of the Federation of Canadian Artists. His work has received awards in international exhibitions, and he currently runs an art studio in Richmond.
